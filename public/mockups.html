The day we left Caracas
January 14, 2026 ✦ Premium

"I remember the weight of the suitcase — not from clothes, but from everything we were leaving behind. Three generations of family, distilled into 23 kilograms."

What came next was something I had rehearsed a hundred times in my mind, but nothing prepares you for the moment the door closes and you know you are not coming back. My mother held my hand at the checkpoint, her grip tighter than I had ever felt it.

The airport was different that day. Quieter in the way a room goes quiet when everyone knows something is ending. I watched the city shrink through the window and felt something I still don't have a word for in any language.
